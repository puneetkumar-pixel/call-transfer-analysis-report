Key Insights & Recommendations

Actionable improvements ranked by potential impact
1
Fix Call Drops — 16 calls lost (35.6% of non-transfers) This is the single biggest issue. 16 calls ended abruptly due to apparent technical issues (call drops, audio cuts, incomplete transcripts). Many of these customers had positive sentiment and were cooperating. At least 7 of these had positive sentiment and were likely to transfer if the call hadn't dropped. Fixing this alone could increase transfers from 15 to ~22, a 47% improvement.
2
Lead Deduplication — 12 calls wasted (26.7% of non-transfers) 12 customers said they already spoke with someone or already have a quote. These leads should not have been called again. Improve CRM sync to flag leads that have already been contacted or have active quotes. This wastes agent time and creates negative customer experience.
3
Add Callback Scheduling — 4 leads recoverable 4 customers said they were busy. The AI ends the call without offering to schedule a callback, losing the lead entirely. Adding a "When would be a good time to call back?" option could recover these leads.
4
Lead Freshness Filtering — 3 stale leads 3 customers had already cancelled their move or decided not to use a moving service. Filter out cancelled/stale leads before dialing to avoid wasted calls and poor customer experience.
5
AI Disclosure Strategy — 1 call lost 1 customer disconnected after learning they were talking to AI ("Are you a real person?"). Consider proactively disclosing AI nature earlier, or improving the transition to human agent for customers who express discomfort with AI.
+
What's Working Well When customers are receptive (positive sentiment), the AI successfully transfers 13 out of 13 times (100% success rate). The script is effective — it confirms details, asks for transfer permission, and connects smoothly. The "Did you make a down payment yet?" objection handler also shows good persistence.
