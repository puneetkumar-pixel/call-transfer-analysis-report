💡 Key Insights & Recommendations
Voicemail Dominance: ~67% of answered calls reach voicemail. Consider optimizing call timing (avoid early morning/late evening) and implementing a voicemail-drop strategy with callback scheduling.
Call Drop-offs Are Significant: In Jan 26-29 data, 605 of 724 non-voicemail calls (83.6%) were customer drop-offs. In Feb 9 data, 16 of 45 non-transferred engaged calls dropped/cut off. Investigate SIP timeout settings and consider shorter intro scripts.
Already-Quoted Customers: 12 of 45 non-transferred Feb 9 calls (26.7%) said they already had a quote. Implement lead deduplication and recency filtering to avoid calling customers already in the pipeline.
Feb 9 Showed Improvement: Answer rate improved from 78.2% to 81.7%, voicemail rate dropped from 68.8% to 60.2%, and transfer rate rose from 1.3% to 2.5%. The AI agent refinements are showing positive trends.
Hostile/Negative Interactions: 13 hostile/DNC cases in Jan 26-29 and several negative interactions in Feb 9. Implement faster detection of negative sentiment with graceful call termination to protect brand reputation.

💡 Sentiment Insights
Transferred calls show dramatically better sentiment: 87% positive sentiment for transferred calls vs. only 16% for non-transferred. This confirms the AI agent is effectively qualifying and routing the most engaged prospects.
Non-transferred calls are predominantly negative (62%): The main drivers are customers who already have quotes (frustrated by repeat calls) and those who explicitly decline. Improving lead filtering would reduce negative interactions.
Jan vs Feb sentiment comparison: Jan 26-29 real contacts were 16.3% positive, 37% neutral, 46.7% negative. Feb 9 transferred calls achieved 87% positive, suggesting the AI script refinements significantly improved customer experience for qualified leads.
